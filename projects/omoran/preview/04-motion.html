04 · I Motion confident, quick · numbers tick, panels slide

Motion is confident but never bouncy in chrome. Two ideas carry it: AI insights slide in from below when the analyst surfaces something (it's arriving, not jumping at you); and live numbers tick — they count to their new value, they don't spring.

Three curves
Standard
cubic-bezier(.2,.7,.2,1)
Hovers, button presses, toggles. The everyday curve.
Arrive
cubic-bezier(.16,1,.3,1)
Insights, drawers, popovers entering. A long ease-out — settles, doesn't overshoot.
Tick
stepped · per-frame
Live counts. The value steps toward its target — a meter, not a spring.

What's allowed, what isn't
  • Allowed: slide-up arrivals for insights and drawers · ticking numbers · 90ms hover feedback · a single live pulse on a connected SDK.
  • Not allowed: bouncy springs in chrome · gratuitous parallax · charts that animate every re-render · anything that delays the operator reading a number.
  • Reduced motion: all transitions collapse to near-instant; the insight simply appears; the counter snaps to its value.
