04MotionFoundation · calm, with one mechanical exception
Three named curves
Settle
cubic-bezier(.2,.7,.3,1) · 300ms
Cards arrive, sheets fade in, the readiness number counts up. The default — long and soft, never abrupt. Fades dominate over slides.
Key
cubic-bezier(.2,.7,.3,1) · 90ms
The one fast, mechanical curve — only the button press. Snappy with a hint of overshoot so it feels like a real key, not a fade.
Tick
linear · per-second
The live test clock. It does not animate; it ticks, one second at a time, and pulses crimson in the final 30 seconds.
Live — press the key, watch it land

Each button sits raised on its own coloured edge. Hover lifts it 1px toward your finger; click drops it the full 3px to land. This is the system's single tactile flourish — everything else stays calm.

Live — the clock in its final seconds
08:24left 00:19left Steady aubergine dot while there's time; pulsing crimson when the clock is the thing that matters most.
Reduced motion

When the operating system asks for reduced motion, the key press becomes an instant colour change, the clock stops pulsing, and all fades collapse to immediate. Nothing essential is ever carried by motion alone — on a flaky mid-range phone, motion is a courtesy, never load-bearing.
